# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fellow designers and creative coders arriving via a shared link. They judge in seconds and by craft: the landing page is itself a work sample. Secondary: Till, using it as the daily index to his own toys.

## Product Purpose

A public playground of self-built generative design tools (kinetic type, flow-field ink, halftone, reaction-diffusion, physics type). Every tool is a single dependency-free HTML file. The landing page's job: make a designer want to click into a toy within five seconds, and demonstrate the playground's quality by being generative itself.

## Brand Personality

Quiet index, live work. The gallery stays dark, terse and understated (small-caps title, muted copy, minimal chrome); the generative previews carry all the energy. Show, never announce: no "poke me" prompts, no credits or self-description beyond one line. Confirmed by Till after rejecting a louder "toy lab" direction (2026-07-20).

## Anti-references

- Loud "playful" landing pages (bubbly copy, sticker cards, hopping letters): tried and rejected
- SaaS landing grammar (hero metric, feature cards, eyebrow labels)
- Static screenshot galleries: previews must be live canvases
- Explanatory copy that names inspirations or narrates the page

## Design Principles

- **Self-demonstrating**: the page runs the tools it links to. Nothing is a mockup.
- **Toys, not products**: labels and copy sound like a playground, not a catalogue.
- **Ink & paper everywhere**: the shared two-color system of the tools is the identity of the page.
- **One file, no dependencies**: the landing page obeys the same rule as every tool.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast for all text
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: previews freeze on their first drawn frame; no autoplaying motion
- All previews are decorative canvases behind real, focusable text links
